Phase 1: Immediate Cleanup

ASAP - Mid May

Bounce Purge Implementation

Hard Bounces

Invalid addresses from last 6-12 months

Consistent Soft Bounces

Addresses that consistently bounce (>3 times in 6 months)

Segment & combine these lists in Constant Contact

Review for any obvious anomalies

Change status to "Unsubscribed" or "Suspended"

Target: Reduce Bounce Rate to < 2% (Currently ~6.5%)
Drastically reduces high bounce rates for improved deliverability
Industry Best Practice: Consider using a 3rd-party email validation service (e.g., ZeroBounce, NeverBounce) for a one-time deep scan before Phase 2 to catch typos, invalid formats, and risky addresses missed by bounce filtering.

Mid/Late May

Identify Unengaged Contacts

Segment contacts with no opens or clicks in last 6 months

Re-Engagement Campaign

Send 1-2 emails asking to confirm subscription

Recommended

Purge Non-Responders

Unsubscribe contacts who didn't take positive action

Remove chronically unengaged contacts (no opens/clicks in 6+ months).
These contacts lower overall engagement rates, send negative signals to Google (like "deletes without opening"), hurt sender reputation, and may harbor hidden spam traps.

Phase 2.5: Re-Warm Sending Reputation

After Cleaning: Gradually Ramp Up Again

  • After major list cleaning (Phases 1 & 2), our sending volume & list composition will shift.
  • Crucial Step: Gradually ramp up email volume again.
  • Start by sending only to the most engaged segments (opened/clicked recently).
  • Slowly increase volume over several weeks, monitoring Google Postmaster Tools closely.
  • This process rebuilds trust with Gmail/ISPs based on the new, cleaner list.
Why?
Prevents sudden reputation shocks and supports better inbox placement after a major clean.
